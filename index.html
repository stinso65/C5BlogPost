Ochako Crochet Pattern

Obligatory Intros:

Hello! My name is Tiara and as you may can tell by this website, I love to crochet! I have been crocheting since 2011 and have created a lot of cool things from patterns and from scratch (a little bit of reverse-engineering from a reference picture goes a long way). Alongside crochet, I like to go to a local anime convention that usually takes place every February. This year (2024), at the last minute (about 3 weeks before the con) I decided to cosplay as Ochako from My Hero Academia/Boku no Hero Academia. To compliment the cosplay, I decided (at 3am) to crochet an amigurumi (small, stuffed toys much like a stuffed animal/character) bag! I placed my big order of Bernat Blanket yarn (because it will take A LOT less time than crocheting with medium weight yarn), galaxy fabric (to line the inside of the body), a bag of polyfil, and a 14” zipper, and practically ran (drove—there is no way I am running to a store 20 miles away) to JoAnn as soon as they opened and completed my order.

With my materials in hand and approximately 2 weeks before the con started, I was ready to crochet her! Speaking of materials, you will need:

Materials:

  • 9MM crochet hook
  • 2 skeins of Big Twist Plush in the color coconut
  • 1 skein of Bernat Blanket yarn in the following colors: baby pink, twilight, sparkle in hot pink
  • 2 skeins of Loops & Threads Chenille Home in the color CH04 (brown)
  • Yarn/Darning needle (the metal curved one works best for this project)
  • PolyFil
  • 1/2 yard of fabric (I chose galaxy print since it fits the theme of the character)
  • Jumbo safety eyes (or black felt)
  • Optional: light brown (matching the hair color) and light pink felt for the eyebrows and blush

I Did the Frustrating Part So You Don't Have To:

Originally, I planned to make the Ochako bag like the official Funko Pop Plush being sold in stores and online on Crunchyroll. That version has a squarer head shape and a smaller body, so I thought that it would be perfect to make the head a “storage” compartment and sew a zipper to open/close it on the back of the head underneath the hair. There were two issues with that:

  • The head would need structural support (I have tried lining and sewing the head with canvas fabric—what a waste of time that was!)
  • It did not look right to me once I made the head into a square shape. So, I settled on the (much easier) traditional round shape

Now that I have gone through the trials and errors, I started making the final project!

Abbreviations:

  • Rnd/rnds-round(s)
  • Sc-single crochet
  • Inc-increase
  • St/sts-stitch(es)
  • BLO-back loops only

Pattern:

Starting with the head in the color coconut:

Rnd 1: 8 sc in magic ring.
Rnd 2: *inc in each st* around.
Rnd 3: *inc, sc* around.
Rnd 4: sc, *inc, sc in next 2sts* sc around.
Rnd 5: *inc, sc in next 3 sts* around.
Rnd 6: sc in next 2 sts, *inc, sc in next 4 sts*, sc in next 2 sts around.
Rnd 7: *inc, sc in next 5 sts* around.
Rnd 8: sc in next 3 sts, *inc, sc in next 6 sts*, sc in next 3 sts around.
Rnd 9: *inc, sc in the next 7 sts* around.
Rnds 10-16: *sc in each st* around.
Rnd 17: sc in next 3 sts, dec, *sc in next 6 sts, dec* around, sc in next 3 sts.
Rnd 18: *sc in next 5 sts, dec* around.
Rnd 19: sc in next 2 sts, dec, *sc in next 4 sts, dec* around, sc in next 2 sts.
Rnd 20: *sc in next 3 sts, dec* around.
Rnd 21 BLO: sc (place a stitch marker on the other loop of the first stitch), *dec, sc in next 2 sts* around, sc.
Do the next rnds AFTER assembling the hair piece, eyes, eyebrows, and blush.
Stuff with polyfill.
Rnd 22: *dec, sc* around.
Rnd 23: *dec* around.
Add more polyfil if necessary.
FO and leave a long tail for sewing the remaining sts closed *OR* keep dec around until it closes and FO (I chose the latter).
Rnd 1: Returning to the loops left on rnd 21, *sc in each st* around (36 sts).
FO. Sew the left (or right) half of the zipper to the inside of rnd 1.
